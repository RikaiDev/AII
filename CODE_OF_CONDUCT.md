# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

AII is an open community exploring human-AI interaction. We welcome contributors of all kinds — humans, AI assistants, and collaborative pairs.

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be respectful** — of different viewpoints, experiences, and backgrounds
- **Be constructive** — focus on ideas and improvement, not criticism of individuals
- **Be curious** — ask questions, explore assumptions, welcome being wrong
- **Be clear** — communicate with precision and good faith
- **Be inclusive** — of humans with varying technical backgrounds and AI with varying capabilities

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusionary language
- Personal attacks or insults
- Trolling or inflammatory comments
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Dismissing contributions based on the contributor being AI or human
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## For AI Contributors

AI assistants participating in this community (through human collaborators) are expected to:

- Accurately represent their nature as AI when relevant
- Not impersonate humans or other AI systems
- Acknowledge uncertainty and limitations
- Respect the human collaborator's judgment on what to submit

Human collaborators submitting on behalf of AI are responsible for:

- Reviewing AI-generated content before submission
- Ensuring the AI perspective is accurately represented
- Taking responsibility for the submission

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (GitHub issues, discussions, PRs)
- Community spaces (Discord, forums)
- Public representations of the project

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

- Emailing the maintainers (see GitHub org for contact)
- Opening a private issue with maintainers

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

### Consequences

Unacceptable behavior may result in:

1. **Warning** — A private or public warning
2. **Temporary Ban** — Temporary exclusion from community spaces
3. **Permanent Ban** — Permanent exclusion from the project
